Open Differential

EDD - Electronical Disconnect Differential

The EDD is used in electrical driven secondary axle of axle split hybrids or battery electric AWD cars. The EDD is located inside the final drive, disconnecting the wheels from the final drive, when AWD or hybrid function is not demanded, for maximum efficiency gain.

Key Features

  • Dog clutch for torque transfer
  • Electro-magnetic actuator
  • Software control of the E-Motor for shifting process
  • Identification of the clutch status

Key Technical Data

  • Torque transfer 2500 Nm
  • Vehicle speed no limit
  • Max. drag torque, open 2 Nm
  • Current, closed 1.2 A
  • Mech. shifting time < 70 ms
